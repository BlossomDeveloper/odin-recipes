Mexican Lasagna Recipe

A homemade Mexican Lasagna

Mexican Lasagna - No Lasagna Noodles!

This is a mexican traditional way to do a lasagna. Anyone can do it ! It is composed of traditional Mexican ingredients layered over the top of one another in a lasagna-like fashion (hence the name) but has a little similarity to lasagna, so lasagna haters have no fear!

ingredients:

  • 1 1/2pounds ground beef
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 (1 ounce) packet taco seasoning mix
  • 12 (8 inch) flour tortillas, or more if needed
  • 1 (14 ounce) can refried beans
  • 3 cups shredded Colby-Jack cheese
  • 1 (8 ounce) jar taco sauce
  • 1 cup shredded Colby-Jack cheese

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and stir in ground beef. Cook and stir until beef is crumbly, evenly browned, and no longer pink, about 10 minutes. Drain and discard any excess grease. Add water and taco seasoning; cook and stir until mixture has thickened, 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Arrange 3 tortillas in the bottom of the casserole dish; spread about half the can of refried beans and the half ground beef atop the tortilla layer. Sprinkle 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese over ground beef layer. Arrange 3 more tortillas over the cheese . Spread the remaining 1/2 can refried beans and the half the jar of taco sauce; sprinkle with 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese. Arrange 3 more tortillas and top with remaining ground beef and 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese. Top with last 3 tortillas and remaining 1/2 jar taco sauce.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until top tortilla layers begins to brown, 20 to 25 minutes.
  5. Turn on oven's broiler.
  6. Place lasagna under the broiler until Colby-Jack cheese is melted and bubbling, 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Enjoy!!!
