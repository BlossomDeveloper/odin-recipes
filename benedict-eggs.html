Benedict Eggs

Benedict Eggs

Hot buttered English muffins, Canadian-style bacon, and poached eggs are topped with a heavenly drizzle of hollandaise sauce. Wonderful for Easter, Mother's Day, or anytime you want to treat yourself to the best brunch in the world! Serve with roasted potatoes for mopping up the extra egg yolk and hollandaise.

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs yolks
  • 3 1/2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 pinch ground white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of water
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 8 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • 8 strips Canadian-style bacon
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened

Steps:

  1. To make hollandaise sauce: Fill the bottom of a doubler boiler part-way with water. Make sure that water does not touch the top pan. Bring the water to a gentle simmer. In the top of the double boiler, whisk together the egg yolks, the lemon juice, white pepper, Worcestershire sauce, and 1 tablespoon water.
  2. Add the melted butter to the egg yolk mixture 1 or 2 tablespoons at a time while whisking yolks constantly. If hollandaise begins to get too thick, ad a teaspoon or two of hot water. Continue whisking until all butter is incorporated. Whisk in salt, then remove from the heat. Place a lid on the pan to keep sauce warm.
  3. Preheaten oven on broiler setting. To Pouch Eggs: Fill a large saucepan with 3 inches of water. Bring the water to a gentle simmer , the add the vinegar. Carefully break the eggs into simmering water, and allow to cook for 2 1/2 to 3 minutes. Yolks should still be soft in center. Remove the eggs from the water with a slotted spoon and set on a warm plate.
  4. While eggs are poaching, brown the bacon in a medium skillet over medium-high heat and toast the English muffins on a baking sheet under the broiler.
  5. Spread toasted muffins with softened butter, and top each one with a slice of bacon, followed by a pouched egg. Place 2 muffins on each plate and drizzle with the hollandaise sauce. Sprinkle with chopped chives and serve immediately!
  6. If you prefer, you can substitute ham for Canadian bacon in this recipe!
